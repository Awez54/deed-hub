PROCEDURE

Step 1


Visit the Sub-Registrar Office:

Go to the local office to check if the land is legit.
Look at where the property is and check its ownership status and pending bills.
Make sure the legal document says the owner's name and check records for the last 30 years for any issues.

Step 2

Buyer's Lawyer Makes Sale Deed:
Your lawyer makes the final sale deed on green paper.
The document is submitted for stamping after leaving the date and place blank.

Step 3


Pay Stamp Duty:

Go to a specific bank to pay stamp duty for the sale deed.
The bank gives you a receipt and marks the sale deed as 'stamp duty received'.
Stamp duty rates: 3% for properties up to INR 45 lakh, 5% for over INR 45 lakh, plus a 1% registration charge.

Step 4

Online Stamp Duty Payment:

Visit Khajane-2 or K2 website to create a challan for stamp duty and registration charges.
Fill in your details and property info, select payment mode, and submit the form.
Get a challan number valid for 90 days. Complete property registration within this time.

Step 5

Execute the Final Deed:

Finalize the deed at the local Sub-Registrar's office with buyer, seller, and witnesses.
The document is registered, and a unique number is assigned.
Pay a registration fee, usually 1% of the transaction value or a maximum of Rs 30,000.



Step 6

Apply for Title Deed Mutation:

Apply for title deed mutation at the City Survey and Land Records Department.
Submit a signed application, affidavit, indemnity bond, and notarized sale deed copy
. The department assesses your request, determines property tax, and issues a mutation letter

Step 7


Confirmation of Ownership:

Once mutation is approved, you officially become the property owner.
The process takes about a month, and the associated costs include an application fee of Rs 100, Rs 200 for the indemnity bond, Rs 100 for the affidavit, and Rs 50 for notary charges.
Due to the complexity, consider seeking help from a property agent, consultant, and a qualified lawyer throughout the process.
